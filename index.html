Who Owns the Peak Power Plants in New York City?

Use the dropdown to view peaker plants of a certain owner

Harlem River Yard

This South Bronx power plant has been open since 2001 and has a 94 MW capacity. The plant has two gas turbines and, along with the nearby Hell Gate powerplant, raised controversy when local residents asked for a timeline for its closure.

Hell Gate

Like the nearby Harlem River Yard plant, the Hell Gate plant has been open since 2001 and has a 94 MW capacity. Community members recently asked the New York State Department of Public Service to reject a NYPA clean energy contract until the Power Authority would commit to an expedited timeline for both plants' closure.

J.J. Seymour

The Brooklyn-based Joseph J. Seymour plant has a capacity of 94 MW, across two 47 MW natural gas turbines.

Kent

Kent has a capacity of 47 MW.

Pouch Terminal

Pouch Terminal has a 47 MW capacity.

Vernon Blvd

This 94 MW capacity plant emits 3.3 tons of NOx and about 32,000 tons of carbon dioxide annually.

Astoria Generating Station

The city's largest peaker plant at 943 MW, the Astoria plant is the city's second largest carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide producer.

Gowanus

In 2021, Eastern Generation ended efforts to replace and rebuild units at Gowanus following opposition from the PEAK Coalition and other local representatives. The Gowanus plant has a 640 MW capacity.

Narrows

The 352 MW Narrows Generating Station is located in Brooklyn.

59th Street

This 17 MW plant in Manhattan has been open for over 50 years, but is planned for decommissioning.

74th Street

This 37 MW plant tops the list of plants with the highest carbon dioxide output rate, at 2.1 tons per MWh. The Manhattan plant is scheduled to be decommissioned.

East River

The East River plant in Manhattan has a capacity of 716 MW.

Hudson Avenue

This 49 MW plant is located in Brooklyn. Con Edison plans to decommission one 16 MW unit of the plant.

Ravenswood

Three units at Ravenswood, totaling 69 MW, are scheduled for decommissioning. The plant has a total capacity of 523 MW and is located in Queens.

Arthur Kill

One 20 MW unit of this 896 MW capacity plant is scheduled for decommissioning. The plant is one of the city's largest and its biggest polluter, producing both the most carbon dioxide and the most nitrous oxide.

Astoria Gas Turbines

The Astoria Gas Turbines has a total capacity of 655 MW. 240 MW of the location are scheduled to be decommissioned.
